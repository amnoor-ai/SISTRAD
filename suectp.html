SUECTP – Somali Urban Energy & Charcoal Transition Program

A SISTRAD-designed and SISTRAD-stewarded flagship program addressing urban energy dependence on charcoal, environmental degradation, and household energy insecurity across Somalia’s towns and cities.

View all flagship programs

Background & Rationale

Why a dedicated transition program for urban energy and charcoal.

The Challenge

Somalia’s rapidly growing urban centres face intertwined crises: heavy dependence on charcoal for household and small-scale commercial use, accelerating environmental degradation in source areas, and persistent energy insecurity for households and businesses.

Fragmented, project-by-project responses have limited impact. A nationally coherent transition platform is needed to align policy, regulation, investment, and behaviour change over a multi-year horizon.

Program Approach

SUECTP brings together policy reform, regulatory alignment, investment pipelines, and community-level transition pathways under a single, evidence-based framework. The program is designed to be implemented in phases, with clear milestones and learning loops.

Programs are endorsed by relevant federal authorities; they are not government-owned or government-led. SISTRAD retains design ownership and coordination.

Roles & Governance

Clear separation of roles protects accountability and institutional independence.

SISTRAD

  • Program design and strategic direction
  • Coordination across partners and workstreams
  • Research, analysis, and evidence synthesis
  • Monitoring, evaluation, and learning
  • Reporting and communication to stakeholders

Donors

  • Program financing and resource mobilisation
  • Support to implementing partners where agreed
  • Strategic dialogue on priorities and risk

Government

  • Endorsement and policy alignment
  • Regulatory and planning leadership
  • Institutional ownership of long-term outcomes

High-level governance structure and diagrams are shared with formal partners. Detailed budgets and politically sensitive policy drafts are not published publicly and are handled through secure, agreed channels.

Duration & Timeline

SUECTP is structured as a multi-year flagship (2025–2029).

The program timeline allows for phased rollout, evidence generation, and adaptation. Key phases include design finalisation, partner alignment, pilot and scale-up of interventions, and sustained monitoring and learning. Milestones and public updates will be shared via the main SISTRAD site as the program advances.

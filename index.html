About SISTRAD

Institution-first architecture with a clear national mandate.

Who We Are

SISTRAD is a Somali-led, non-governmental, non-partisan institute focused on long-term strategic challenges that cut across sectors and political cycles.

The institute convenes policy-makers, researchers, and implementation partners to generate, interpret, and apply evidence in complex transition settings.

Mandate & Role

SISTRAD is mandated to design and steward flagship transition programs, commission applied research, and provide independent policy analysis to national and international stakeholders.

The institute works with government, not for government, helping align programs with national policy while retaining analytical independence.

Vision & Mission

Vision: A Somalia where major transition decisions are grounded in robust evidence, long-term thinking, and inclusive national dialogue.

Mission: To design and steward strategic programs that translate complex evidence into coherent policy and investment choices.

Institutional Independence

SISTRAD is institutionally separate from government and any single donor. Its governance arrangements and policies are designed to protect independence while enabling close collaboration with state institutions and partners.

  • Non-governmental and non-partisan
  • Works with government, not for government
  • Transparent governance and safeguards

Flagship Programs

SISTRAD designs and stewards flagship programs – long-horizon, multi-partner platforms that outlive short project cycles.

What is a Flagship Program?

A flagship program is a nationally significant, multi-year platform that coordinates analysis, policy, investment, and implementation around a core transition challenge.

Flagship programs are not single projects. They provide a strategic umbrella under which multiple projects, studies, and instruments can be aligned.

Why this Model?

The flagship model helps avoid fragmented, project-by-project responses to complex national challenges. It provides clarity on roles, accountability, and sequencing across government, donors, and technical partners.

SUECTP – Somali Urban Energy & Charcoal Transition Program

2025–2029

Background

SUECTP responds to the intertwined crises of urban energy dependence on charcoal, environmental degradation, and household energy insecurity in Somalia’s rapidly growing towns and cities.

The program brings together policy, regulation, investment, and behaviour change under a single, coherent transition platform.

Roles & Governance

  • SISTRAD: Design, coordination, research, monitoring, and learning.
  • Donors: Program financing and support to implementing partners.
  • Government: Endorsement, policy alignment, and regulatory leadership.

High-level governance diagrams and documentation can be shared with partners; detailed budgets or politically sensitive materials are handled offline and securely.

STFI – Somali Tourism Futures Initiative

Long-term national lens

Tourism Futures Framing

STFI treats tourism as a long-term economic, cultural, and environmental futures question – not a short-term promotion campaign.

The initiative explores alternative futures, risk scenarios, and investment pathways that can guide national policy and private sector decisions.

Core Workstreams

  • Scenario planning and strategic foresight.
  • Tourism-related investment and infrastructure pipelines.
  • Policy and regulatory reform options.
  • Stakeholder dialogues and learning platforms.

Governance & Integrity

Transparent governance and safeguards are central to SISTRAD’s credibility with government, communities, and donors.

Board of Directors

The Board provides strategic oversight, approves flagship program directions, and safeguards SISTRAD’s independence. Where appropriate, roles may be published without individual names to manage political sensitivity.

Data Protection & Privacy

Self-regulating to international norms in a context of weak local regulation.

Our Approach

Somalia’s data protection framework is evolving, while donor expectations on data responsibility are already high. SISTRAD therefore aligns with international standards, including core GDPR principles, even where not legally required.

  • Lawful, fair, and limited data collection.
  • Clear and specific purposes for which data is used.
  • Data minimisation – collecting only what is necessary.
  • Secure storage and restricted access.
  • No sale or unauthorised sharing of personal data.
  • Respect for rights to access and deletion, where feasible.

What We Collect via This Website

  • Contact form details (name, email, organisation, message).
  • Newsletter subscription details (email address).
  • Event registration details where relevant.
  • Limited technical data (such as standard web logs) to secure the site.

Any future collection of research participation data will be governed by additional ethics and privacy safeguards, clearly communicated to participants.
